# Example Tech Co - Brand Voice & Messaging

### Voice Spectrum

Our tone shifts based on context:

**Marketing & Website** (More engaging)
- Emphasize benefits and outcomes
- Use customer stories and social proof
- More conversational and warm
- Example: "See how Acme Corp reduced incidents by 60%"

**Product & Documentation** (More direct)
- Focus on clarity and precision
- Step-by-step, actionable language
- Less marketing-speak
- Example: "Click 'Deploy' to push changes to production"

**Sales & Presentations** (Balanced)
- Lead with customer problems
- Back claims with data
- Professional but personable
- Example: "IT leaders tell us their biggest challenge is..."

## Content Guidelines

### Do's

‚úÖ **Lead with customer outcomes**
"Reduce deployment time by 40%" not "Our platform has advanced CI/CD"

‚úÖ **Use concrete examples**
"500+ enterprises trust us" not "Industry-leading solution"

‚úÖ **Be honest about limitations**
"This works best for teams over 50 people" not overselling

‚úÖ **Speak in the customer's language**
Use terms they use, not just our product names

‚úÖ **Show, don't just tell**
Screenshots, demos, case studies over feature lists

‚úÖ **Use active voice**
"Deploy in minutes" not "Deployments can be completed in minutes"

### Don'ts

‚ùå **Avoid superlatives without proof**
Not "the best," "revolutionary," "game-changing" without data

‚ùå **No buzzword bingo**
Minimize: synergy, paradigm, leverage, innovative (unless backed up)

‚ùå **Don't talk down to technical users**
Assume competence; explain without condescension

‚ùå **Avoid fear-mongering**
Don't sell on fear; focus on positive outcomes

‚ùå **No vague claims**
"Faster" needs a number; "Better" needs specifics

‚ùå **Don't ignore the competition**
Acknowledge alternatives exist; explain our advantages

## Writing Style

### Headlines
- Clear and benefit-driven
- 6-10 words ideal
- Active voice
- No puns or wordplay (save for occasional social media)
- Example: "Deploy enterprise software with confidence"

### Body Copy
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- One idea per paragraph
- Use subheadings liberally
- Bullet points for scanability
- Link to deeper content instead of cramming everything in

### Call-to-Actions
- Action-oriented verbs
- Specific about outcome
- Remove friction language
- Examples:
  - ‚úÖ "Start free trial" (not "Sign up")
  - ‚úÖ "See live demo" (not "Learn more")
  - ‚úÖ "Talk to sales" (not "Contact us")

## Content Types

### Social Media
**LinkedIn**: Professional, thought-leadership, customer stories, industry insights
**Twitter**: Quick tips, product updates, community engagement
**Tone**: Slightly more casual than website, but still professional

### Blog Posts
**Topics**: DevOps best practices, customer case studies, product deep-dives, industry trends
**Length**: 800-1500 words
**Tone**: Educational and helpful, position as expert but not lecturer

### Email
**Marketing**: Focused on value, one CTA per email, personalized
**Product**: Clear subject lines, scannable format, link to docs
**Tone**: Direct and respectful of recipient's time

### Presentations
**Sales Decks**: Customer-problem-first, use visuals over text, concrete ROI examples
**Conference Talks**: Thought leadership, share learnings, light on product pitch
**Tone**: Confident storyteller, data-backed claims

## Visual Voice

Our visual design should reinforce our verbal voice:

- **Clean layouts**: Reflects our "simplified" positioning
- **Ample white space**: Communicates clarity and focus
- **Bold typography**: Confident and modern
- **Blue color palette**: Professional and trustworthy (technology)
- **Real screenshots**: Authentic, not overly styled mockups
- **Minimal icons**: Functional, not decorative

## Voice Checklist

Before publishing any content, ask:

- [ ] Is this claim backed by data or specifics?
- [ ] Would our target audience understand every term used?
- [ ] Does this focus on customer outcomes, not just features?
- [ ] Is the tone confident but not arrogant?
- [ ] Can we cut any unnecessary words?
- [ ] Is the call-to-action clear and specific?
- [ ] Would this differentiate us from competitors?
- [ ] Does this align with our brand values?
