# Business Card Template

## Asset Specifications

### Standard US Business Card
- **Dimensions**: 3.5 x 2 inches (89 x 51 mm)
- **Digital Dimensions**: 1050 x 600 pixels (300 DPI)
- **Aspect Ratio**: 1.75:1 (horizontal)
- **Bleed**: Add 0.125 inches (3.75mm) on all sides for print
- **Safe Zone**: Keep critical content 0.125 inches from trim edge
- **File Format**:
  - Print: PDF (CMYK, 300 DPI)
  - Digital preview: PNG or JPEG
- **Sides**: Front and back (optional back)

### Other Common Sizes
- **EU Standard**: 85 x 55 mm (3.35 x 2.17 inches)
- **Square**: 2.5 x 2.5 inches
- **Mini**: 3.5 x 1.25 inches (slim)
- **Folded**: 7 x 2 inches (folded to 3.5 x 2)

## Platform Best Practices

### Essential Information
**Front Side (Required):**
- Name (prominently displayed)
- Job title or role
- Company name and/or logo
- Primary contact method (phone or email)

**Back Side (Optional):**
- Additional contact info
- Social media handles
- Website/portfolio URL
- QR code
- Services list
- Mission statement
- Second language translation

### Text Guidelines
- **Name**: 14-18pt, prominent, bold
- **Title**: 10-12pt, clear hierarchy below name
- **Company**: 12-16pt, or use logo
- **Contact Info**: 8-10pt, legible but secondary
- **Minimum Font Size**: 7pt for print legibility
- **Font Choice**: Professional, on-brand, highly legible
- **Text Color**: High contrast with background (4.5:1 minimum)

### Visual Guidelines
- **Logo Placement**: Typically top-left, bottom-left, or center
- **Orientation**: Horizontal (standard) or vertical (distinctive)
- **White Space**: Don't overcrowd - less is more
- **Alignment**: Consistent alignment creates polish
- **Color Strategy**:
  - 1-3 brand colors maximum
  - Consider printing costs (full color vs spot color)
- **Print Considerations**:
  - CMYK color mode (not RGB)
  - 300 DPI minimum resolution
  - Avoid gradients in large areas (printing artifacts)
  - Be careful with very dark backgrounds (ink saturation)

## Front vs Back Strategy

### Option A: Front Only
- All essential info on front
- Clean, uncluttered design
- Cost-effective printing
- Simple and direct

### Option B: Front + Back
**Front**: Brand showcase
- Logo prominent
- Name and title
- Minimal or no contact info
- Visually striking

**Back**: Information
- All contact details
- Social media
- Website/portfolio
- QR code

### Option C: Front + Functional Back
**Front**: Standard card
**Back**: Value-add
- Appointment card format
- Discount code
- Calendar
- Service menu
- Notes area

## What to Avoid

❌ **Too much information**: Cramming every detail makes nothing stand out
❌ **Too many fonts**: Stick to 2 font families maximum
❌ **Low-quality images**: Pixelated logos look unprofessional
❌ **Tiny text**: If people need a magnifying glass, it's too small
❌ **Poor contrast**: Light text on light background is illegible
❌ **Clip art or generic graphics**: Use original brand elements
❌ **Trendy effects**: Excessive gradients, shadows, or effects date quickly
❌ **No hierarchy**: Every element same size creates visual chaos
❌ **Forgetting bleed**: Designs will be cut wrong without proper setup

## Design Tips

✅ **Embrace white space** - Breathing room makes designs elegant
✅ **Create hierarchy** - Name most important, then title, then contact info
✅ **Align consistently** - Pick left, right, or center and stick with it
✅ **Use brand colors strategically** - Accent colors for emphasis
✅ **Consider texture** - Matte, glossy, textured stock affects perception
✅ **Think about the hand-off** - Easy to read while someone holds it
✅ **Make it memorable** - Distinctive but not gimmicky
✅ **Print test samples** - Colors look different on screen vs paper
✅ **Keep updated** - Outdated phone numbers undermine credibility
✅ **Design for scanning** - Some people will photograph it, not keep it

## Accessibility Notes

- **Color Contrast**: Minimum 4.5:1 ratio for text (even more important in print)
- **Font Size**: Nothing smaller than 7pt for print
- **Sans-serif for small text**: More legible at small sizes
- **Clear Hierarchy**: Visual order should be obvious
- **Avoid Color-Only Meaning**: Don't rely on color alone to communicate
- **High-Quality Images**: 300 DPI minimum for logos and graphics
- **Consider Colorblind Users**: Test in grayscale

## Print Specifications

### File Setup
- **Color Mode**: CMYK (not RGB)
- **Resolution**: 300 DPI minimum
- **Dimensions**: 3.5 x 2 inches
- **Bleed**: 0.125 inches on all sides (final: 3.75 x 2.25 inches)
- **Safe Zone**: Keep text 0.125 inches from trim edge
- **Format**: PDF (print-ready) with crop marks

### Color Considerations
- **Spot Color**: 1-2 colors, cost-effective
- **Full Color (CMYK)**: More expensive but allows photos/gradients
- **Pantone Colors**: For exact brand color matching
- **Black**: Use rich black (C:60 M:40 Y:40 K:100) not pure black (K:100)

### Paper Stock Options
- **Standard**: 14pt or 16pt cardstock
- **Premium**: 18pt with coating (glossy, matte, silk)
- **Luxury**: Thick stock (24pt+), textured, colored, letterpress
- **Specialty**: Plastic, metal, wood, die-cut shapes
- **Eco-Friendly**: Recycled or sustainable materials

## Content Planning Questions

When creating a business card, ask:
1. **What's the primary goal?** (networking, sales, credibility, branding)
2. **What's the one thing people should remember?** (name, company, unique offering)
3. **Who receives these cards?** (clients, partners, event attendees, potential employers)
4. **What contact method do you prefer?** (email, phone, LinkedIn, website)
5. **What's your industry standard?** (conservative vs creative)
6. **Do you need a back?** (enough info to warrant it?)
7. **Any special requirements?** (second language, credentials, QR code)
8. **Print budget?** (affects stock, finishing, quantity)

## Technical Implementation

### HTML Structure for Digital Preview
```html
<div style="width: 1050px; height: 600px; position: relative;
            background: white; border: 1px solid #e5e7eb;
            box-shadow: 0 4px 6px rgba(0,0,0,0.1);">

  <!-- Bleed indicator (for reference, not printed) -->
  <div style="position: absolute; inset: 37.5px; border: 1px dashed #ccc; opacity: 0.3;">
    <!-- Safe zone indicator -->
  </div>

  <!-- Actual card content (within safe zone) -->
  <div style="position: absolute; inset: 75px; display: flex;
              flex-direction: column; justify-content: space-between;">

    <!-- Logo -->
    <div>
      <img src="[logo-path]" style="height: 60px;" alt="Company logo">
    </div>

    <!-- Name and title -->
    <div style="margin-top: 80px;">
      <h1 style="font-size: 48px; font-weight: 700; color: [brand-color];
                 line-height: 1.1; margin-bottom: 8px;">
        John Doe
      </h1>
      <p style="font-size: 32px; color: #6b7280; font-weight: 500;">
        Creative Director
      </p>
    </div>

    <!-- Contact info -->
    <div style="font-size: 24px; color: #374151; line-height: 1.6;">
      <div>john@email.com</div>
      <div>+1 (555) 123-4567</div>
      <div>website.com</div>
    </div>

  </div>

</div>
```

### Back Side (if applicable)
```html
<div style="width: 1050px; height: 600px; position: relative;
            background: [brand-color]; color: white;">

  <!-- Back content - could be QR code, additional info, or pattern -->
  <div style="position: absolute; inset: 75px; display: flex;
              align-items: center; justify-content: center;">

    <!-- Example: QR code centered -->
    <div style="text-align: center;">
      <div style="background: white; padding: 20px; border-radius: 8px;">
        <!-- QR code would go here -->
        <div style="width: 200px; height: 200px; background: #e5e7eb;"></div>
      </div>
      <p style="margin-top: 16px; font-size: 20px;">
        Scan to connect
      </p>
    </div>

  </div>

</div>
```

### Export Settings
- **For Digital Preview**: PNG, 1050 x 600px, 72-144 DPI
- **For Print**: PDF, 3.75 x 2.25 inches (with bleed), CMYK, 300 DPI, outlined fonts

## Print Production Notes

### Pre-Flight Checklist
- [ ] All text converted to outlines (or fonts embedded)
- [ ] Images are 300 DPI and CMYK
- [ ] Bleed is set correctly (0.125" all sides)
- [ ] No critical content in safe zone margins
- [ ] PDF is print-ready (not web-optimized)
- [ ] Colors match brand standards (Pantone or CMYK values)
- [ ] Proofread all text (names, numbers, emails)
- [ ] Test printed at actual size

### Working with Printers
- Request physical proof if possible
- Ask about paper stock samples
- Confirm turnaround time and quantity
- Check for coating options (matte, gloss, spot UV)
- Inquire about special finishes (foil, embossing, die-cut)
- Get quote for 500, 1000, and 2500 quantities
